About

My work focuses on vision-transparent tactile sensing and safe, precise manipulation. I build end-to-end systems—from sensor design and calibration, to perception models, controller integration, and rigorous evaluation. Typical scenarios include cap unscrewing, small-part assembly, and handling containers with liquid, where maintaining line-of-sight while reading 6-DoF force/torque and contact semantics is critical.

Research Focus

Visuotactile Sensing

Forces & Contact Semantics

Dexterous & Safe Manipulation

Perception-to-Action

Selected Projects

MoiréTac — Vision-Through Visuotactile Sensor

Problem:

Many tactile skins block vision; tasks like cap removal need line-of-sight + torque + slip/tilt simultaneously.

Approach:

Dual-grating optics produce moiré patterns; frequency-domain features + optical flow + physics priors estimate Fz/Tz and contact pose.

Result:

Real-time force/torque cues with maintained visual access to the target.

UltraTac — Ultrasound-Enhanced Tactile

Problem:

Pure tactile struggles on soft/transparent objects and liquid containers.

Approach:

Fuse ultrasound ranging/reflectance with tactile images; temporal features and simple reflectance models.

Result:

More reliable material/state classification and steadier manipulation policies.

LED Waveguide Skin — Self-Explaining Tactile

Idea:

Embedded LEDs + light-guide for on-sensor status visualization (illumination, contact "hot spots", ROI guides) and easier alignment/debug.

Notes:

Brightness uniformity tuning; synchronized prompts with tactile capture.

FlexLock — Variable-Stiffness Arm

Goal:

Reversible soft↔rigid switching for tight spaces and human-shared environments.

Method:

SMA springs + distributed heating with particle damping; stiffness-trajectory planning with dual-sensor feedback.

Demos:

Impact absorption in soft mode; heavy pick/hold after stiffening.
